Investment analysis

Economic Analysis of Energy Investments

Run any energy project — solar, battery, retrofit, efficiency upgrade — through a full engineering-economics suite: NPV, future worth, annual worth, IRR, payback, benefit-cost ratio, plus sensitivity, breakeven and risk/probability analysis.

Project inputs

NPV / Net Present Worth

Discounts all future cash flows to today at your MARR. NPV > 0 means the project beats your required return.

Net Future Worth

The same cash flows compounded to end of life — same signal as NPV, as a future lump sum.

Annual Worth (EUAW)

NPV converted to an equivalent uniform annual amount — ideal for comparing different-length projects.

IRR

The discount rate where NPV = 0. IRR > MARR clears your hurdle.

Incremental ROR

Return on the extra investment when choosing between two options.

Payback Period

Years to recover the outlay — both simple and discounted.

Benefit-Cost Ratio

PV of benefits ÷ PV of costs. BCR > 1 means benefits outweigh costs.

Sensitivity Analysis

How NPV shifts as the discount rate changes — revealing which assumptions matter.

Breakeven Analysis

The annual benefit at which NPV = 0 — your minimum viable saving.

Risk & Probability

A Monte-Carlo spread estimates the probability of positive NPV under benefit uncertainty.
