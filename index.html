Get more value

Performance & Value Insights

Two kinds of advice. First, how to lift raw generation. Second — and where the real money is — how to capture more value from the energy you already produce. Your benchmarking result personalises these tips.

🔧

1. Raise generation

Clean panels (soiling can cost 5–20%), clear shading from new growth or aerials, check for under-performing strings or a faulty optimiser, verify inverter isn't clipping, correct tilt/azimuth, and watch for hot-spot or degradation faults flagged when your yield drifts below benchmark.

🏠

2. Self-consume more by day

Shift heavy loads — dishwasher, laundry, pool pump, hot-water element, air-con pre-cooling — into peak sun hours with timers or smart plugs. Every kWh you use yourself is worth your import price, often 3–5× the feed-in price.

🔋

3. Store for the evening

A home battery soaks up midday surplus and powers the evening peak. We size it from your export profile and show the payback so you only buy storage if it actually pays in your tariff.

🚗

4. Charge an EV on sunshine

Daytime EV charging turns ~7c export energy into ~free driving. We estimate solar-miles per day and the petrol/fuel cost you'd avoid.

Low-carbon buildings

Green & Low-Energy Buildings

Tools and guidance to design, certify and operate low-carbon buildings — envelope heat-gain estimators, embodied & operational carbon, certification road-maps, and full ISO 50001 / EnMS implementation support.

Envelope OTTV / RTTV Estimator

Overall Thermal Transfer Value (walls) and Roof Thermal Transfer Value — the heat-gain metric behind BCA Green Mark, GBI and similar tropical/sub-tropical codes. Lower is better.
Formula: OTTV = 15(1−WWR)·α·Uw + 6·WWR·Uf + 194·CF·WWR·SC. Typical code limit ≈ 50 W/m². RTTV uses an analogous roof form.

Embodied & Operational Carbon Calculator

Whole-life carbon: the emissions locked into materials (embodied) plus the emissions from running the building over its life (operational).

Investment analysis

Economic Analysis of Energy Investments

Run any energy project — solar, battery, retrofit, efficiency upgrade — through a full engineering-economics suite: NPV, future worth, annual worth, IRR, incremental ROR, payback, benefit-cost ratio, plus sensitivity, breakeven and risk/probability analysis.

Project inputs

NPV / Net Present Worth

Discounts all future cash flows to today's value at your MARR. NPV > 0 means the project earns more than your required return — accept it.

Net Future Worth

The same cash flows compounded forward to the end of the project life. Same accept/reject signal as NPV, expressed as a future lump sum.

Annual Worth (EUAW)

Converts NPV into an equivalent uniform annual amount — handy for comparing projects of different lengths on a per-year basis.

IRR

The discount rate at which NPV = 0. If IRR > MARR the project clears your hurdle rate.

Incremental ROR

Compares two competing options by analysing the rate of return on the extra investment of the costlier one. Used when choosing between alternatives.

Payback Period

Years to recover the initial outlay. We show both simple and discounted payback.

Benefit-Cost Ratio

Present value of benefits ÷ present value of costs. BCR > 1 means benefits outweigh costs.

Sensitivity Analysis

Shows how NPV moves as a key variable (e.g. discount rate, benefit) changes — revealing which assumptions matter most.

Breakeven Analysis

Finds the value of a variable at which the project just breaks even (NPV = 0), such as the minimum annual saving needed.

Risk & Probability Analysis

A simple Monte-Carlo style spread gives the probability the project delivers a positive NPV under uncertainty in benefits.
